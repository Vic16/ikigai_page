The History of
Aikido

Aikido is more than a martial art; it is a path to harmony and the "Art of Peace." Born from the ancient traditions of Japan and the spiritual awakening of its founder.

合気道

"To control aggression without inflicting injury is the Art of Peace."

— Morihei Ueshiba

Morihei Ueshiba (O-Sensei)

Born in Tanabe, Japan in 1883, Morihei Ueshiba was a man of immense physical strength and deep spiritual curiosity. After mastering several traditional martial arts, including Daito-ryu Aiki-jujutsu, he sought a way to resolve conflict without destruction.

Ueshiba's "enlightenment" moments led him to the realization that true Budo (the martial way) is not about defeating others, but about the protection of all life. This radical philosophy transformed the brutal techniques of the past into the flowing, harmonious movements of modern Aikido.

The Art of Peace

Aikido philosophy is built on three core concepts: Ai (Harmony), Ki (Energy), and Do (Way).

Harmony (Ai)

Blending with an attacker's momentum rather than clashing against it. Harmony turns conflict into a shared resolution.

Energy (Ki)

Connecting with the universal life force. Aikido practitioners learn to extend their energy to lead and control an opponent.

The Way (Do)

A lifelong journey of self-refinement. The dojo is a laboratory for living a more centered, peaceful life in the world.

Chronicle of Aikido

1883 - 1912

The early years of Morihei Ueshiba. Intense training in various Ju-jutsu, Kenjutsu, and Sojutsu styles across Japan.

Foundation

1920s - 1930s

Development of "Aiki-Budo". Ueshiba opens his first dojo in Tokyo, refining his techniques away from combat and toward spiritual growth.

Evolution

1942

The term "Aikido" is officially registered. O-Sensei moves to Iwama to focus on the fusion of farming, training, and prayer.

Naming

Post-War - Today

Global expansion. Aikido spreads to all corners of the world as a tool for peace and international cooperation.
